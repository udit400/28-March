Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about deepfake voice detection, supported audio, and what the report means.

What is deepfake voice detection?
Deepfake voice detection analyzes audio to determine whether a voice was artificially generated or cloned with AI. It looks for unnatural pauses, pitch inconsistencies, robotic tone shifts, and other synthesis artifacts that are difficult to catch by ear.
Is the result 100% accurate?
No AI detector is perfect. This tool gives you a practical risk score and explanation so you can review suspicious clips with better evidence, especially when the audio quality is poor or heavily compressed.
What audio formats are supported?
You can upload common audio formats such as MP3, WAV, AAC, OGG, FLAC, and M4A. Clearer recordings usually lead to more reliable results.
Can this help with scam calls or fake family voices?
Yes. The page is designed for exactly that use case. If you receive a suspicious voicemail, emergency request, or business call recording, you can upload it here and get a quick authenticity signal before responding.
Do you store my audio?
The normal scan flow is intended for immediate analysis rather than permanent storage. Use the copied result text if you want to keep a record of what the detector reported.
