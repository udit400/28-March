Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 28, 2025

 Deepfake Detection is designed with privacy at its core. All analysis runs locally on your machine — your files are never stored, shared, or sent to third-party services.

1. Overview

Deepfake Detection ("we", "our", "the Service") provides AI-powered deepfake detection tools through a web interface and browser extension. This Privacy Policy describes how we handle information when you use our Service.

2. Information We Collect

Account Information: If you create an account, we collect your email address for authentication purposes (OTP login). We do not collect names, phone numbers, or payment card details.

Uploaded Media: When you upload images, videos, or audio files for analysis, the files are processed in-memory on your local backend server. We do not store, retain, or transmit uploaded files. Files are discarded immediately after analysis completes.

Analysis Results: Detection results (scores, verdicts, proof hashes) are returned to your browser and are not stored on any server unless you explicitly save them.

Usage Data: The self-hosted backend does not collect analytics, telemetry, or browsing behavior.

3. How We Use Information

  • To authenticate your identity via one-time passwords sent to your email
  • To process your uploaded media files and return detection results
  • To manage subscription status if you upgrade to a premium plan

4. Data Storage & Security

All data is stored locally in a SQLite database on your machine. Email addresses and OTP hashes are the only persistent data. Media files are never written to disk during analysis.

We use industry-standard security practices including:

  • Bcrypt hashing for OTP codes
  • JWT tokens for authentication with expiration
  • HTTPS encryption (when configured)
  • No third-party tracking scripts or analytics

5. Third-Party Services

By default, the Service runs entirely on your local machine and does not share data with third parties. If the optional Reality Defender API integration is enabled, media data may be sent to Reality Defender's servers for analysis. This is an opt-in feature that requires explicit API key configuration.

The browser extension communicates only with your local backend server (127.0.0.1:8000) and does not send data to any external servers.

6. Browser Extension Permissions

The Deepfake Detection browser extension requests the following permissions:

  • activeTab: To scan video elements on the currently viewed web page
  • scripting: To inject the content script that performs frame capture and displays results
  • storage: To save your authentication token locally in the browser

The extension does not access browsing history, bookmarks, cookies, or any data beyond the active tab's video elements.

7. Cookies

The Service does not use cookies for tracking. Authentication is handled via JWT tokens stored in your browser's localStorage.

8. Data Retention

Account emails remain in the local database until you delete your account. OTP codes expire after 10 minutes and are automatically invalidated. Uploaded files are never persisted.

9. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

10. Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your machine, you have full control:

  • You can delete the SQLite database file to remove all account data
  • You can uninstall the browser extension to remove all extension data
  • You can clear localStorage to remove authentication tokens

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

12. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please reach out via the project's repository or contact page.
